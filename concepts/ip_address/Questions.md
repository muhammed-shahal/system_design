## Q1. How do IPv4 and IPv6 addresses differ?

Ans: IPv4 and IPv6 are the two primary internet protocols. IPv4, with a 32-bit address space, is limited, whereas IPv6, with a 128-bit space, offers a larger pool of addresses and better scalability. IPv6 also improves security with built-in encryption.

## Q2. Why do we need private IPs in system design?

Ans: Private IPs allow organizations to efficiently manage internal networking without requiring a large number of public IPs. They provide security by isolating internal systems and reducing attack exposure

## Q3. How does NAT help in addressing the IPv4 shortage?

Ans: NAT mitigates IPv4 shortages by enabling multiple devices to share a single public IP. It improves security by masking internal IPs and is widely used in enterprise and ISP networks.
